Louisiana as a Lens on Mass Incarceration

Louisiana has the highest incarceration rate in the United States at 1,067 per 100,000 residents. This surpasses not only the national average but also every country in the world. This extreme rate makes Louisiana a revealing case study for understanding systemic issues in the U.S. prison system.

Racial Detention

One major concern is racial disparity. While Black residents make up 32% of Louisiana’s population, they represent 66% of the prison population and 57% of those in local jails. In contrast, white residents are underrepresented in both. These figures reflect broader patterns of racialized incarceration across the country.

Pretrial Detention

Another issue is pretrial detention. Thousands of individuals are held in Louisiana jails without a conviction, often due to inability to afford bail. The number of pretrial detainees has remained consistently high over the past four decades, raising concerns about due process and the criminalization of poverty.

This information comes from the Prison Policy Initiative's profile on Louisiana.
